France

The motto of France is Liberté, Égalité, Fraternité, which translates to "Liberty, Equality, Fraternity".

By: Ayodele Samuel Adebayo (2023/A/SENG/0086)

  • 1. The Eiffel Tower

    The Eiffel Tower, an iconic symbol of Paris, stands at 330 meters tall and attracts nearly 7 million visitors each year, making it one of the most visited monuments in the world. Constructed by Gustave Eiffel for the 1889 Exposition Universelle, this architectural marvel offers breathtaking panoramic views of the city from its observation decks. Beyond sightseeing, visitors can enjoy dining at the tower’s restaurants, exploring interactive exhibits, and experiencing a unique part of French history and culture.

  • 2. The Eiffel Tower

    You should visit Bordeaux, a vibrant port city on the Garonne River in southwestern France, which serves as the capital of the Nouvelle-Aquitaine region. Known for its rich history, stunning architecture, and renowned wine culture, Bordeaux offers visitors a blend of cultural heritage and scenic beauty. Exploring the city and its surrounding region, travelers can experience the unique charm of the “Bordelais” lifestyle and immerse themselves in one of France’s most celebrated destinations.

    Learn more

  • 3. Alsace Region

    You should visit the Alsace region for its enchanting blend of French and German culture, charming medieval villages, and picturesque vineyards. Nestled along the Rhine River, Alsace is famous for its colorful half-timbered houses, scenic wine routes, and Christmas markets that come alive with festive spirit. From exploring historic towns like Strasbourg and Colmar to tasting some of the finest wines in Europe, Alsace offers a unique and memorable experience for any traveler.
